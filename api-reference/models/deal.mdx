---
title: Deal
description: Executed trade deal information
---

The Deal model represents a completed trading transaction in the MT5 system. Deals are the historical record of all trading activity, including position entries, exits, and balance operations.

## Properties

<ResponseField name="ticket" type="integer">
  The unique ticket number identifying this deal. This is the primary identifier for the transaction.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="entry" type="integer">
  The deal entry type indicating how this deal affects positions.
  
  **Allowed values:**
  - `0` - In market (opening a position)
  - `1` - Out of market (closing a position)
  - `2` - Reverse (reversing a position)
  - `3` - Closed by hedged position
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="reason" type="integer">
  The reason why this deal was executed.
  
  **Common values:**
  - `0` - Deal placed manually
  - `1` - Deal placed by expert advisor
  - `2` - Deal placed by dealer
  - `3` - Deal placed due to Stop Loss
  - `4` - Deal placed due to Take Profit
  - `16` - Deal placed manually by mobile terminal
  - `17` - Deal placed manually by web terminal
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="action" type="integer">
  The deal action type.
  
  **Allowed values:**
  - `0` - Buy
  - `1` - Sell
  - `2` - Deposit operation
  - `3` - Credit operation
  - `4` - Additional charges
  - `5` - Correction deals
  - `6` - Bonus
  - `7` - Commission
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="type" type="integer">
  The deal type.
  
  **Allowed values:**
  - `0` - Buy
  - `1` - Sell
  - `4` - Charge
  - `6` - Balance
  - `7` - Commission
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="type_str" type="string">
  A human-readable string representation of the deal type.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="symbol" type="string">
  The trading symbol for this deal (e.g., "EURUSD", "GOLD"). May be empty for balance operations.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="lots" type="float">
  The volume of the deal in lots. For balance operations, this may be 0.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="time" type="string">
  The timestamp when the deal was executed. Typically in datetime string format.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="price" type="float">
  The execution price for the deal. For balance operations, this represents the amount.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="comment" type="string">
  Optional comment or description associated with the deal.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="sl" type="float">
  The Stop Loss price level associated with this deal, if any.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="tp" type="float">
  The Take Profit price level associated with this deal, if any.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="pl" type="float">
  The profit or loss resulting from this deal. Positive values indicate profit.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="commission" type="float">
  The commission charged for this deal.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="swap" type="float">
  The swap (overnight interest) charged or credited for this deal.
</ResponseField>

## Deal types explained

<Expandable title="Understanding entry types">
  - **In market (0)**: Opening a new position or adding to an existing one
  - **Out of market (1)**: Closing a position or reducing its size
  - **Reverse (2)**: Closing current position and opening opposite position
  - **Closed by (3)**: Position closed by an opposite hedging position
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Understanding deal reasons">
  The `reason` field helps you understand why a trade was executed:
  
  - Manual trades (0, 16, 17) - Trader initiated
  - Expert advisor (1) - Automated trading system
  - Stop Loss/Take Profit (3, 4) - Risk management triggered
  - Dealer (2) - Broker intervention
</Expandable>

<Note>
  Deals are immutable historical records. Once created, they cannot be modified. They serve as the audit trail for all account activity.
</Note>
